## Ingredients
- 1 lb ground beef
- 8 taco shells or small tortillas
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/4 cup water
- Toppings: lettuce, tomato, cheese, sour cream, salsa

## Instructions
1. Brown the ground beef over medium-high heat, breaking it into crumbles. Drain excess fat.
2. Stir in chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, paprika, and salt.
3. Add the water and simmer 3–4 minutes until it thickens into a glaze.
4. Warm the shells while the beef finishes.
5. Fill shells with beef and pile on toppings.

## Notes
The splash of water is the trick — it lets the spices bloom and coat the meat evenly.

## Tips
- Toast dry taco shells in a 350°F oven for 3 minutes for extra crunch.
- Double the seasoning batch and jar it for next time.

## Variations
- Use ground turkey or plant-based crumbles.
- Turn leftovers into a taco salad or quesadilla.
